New Zealand: The Capacity Opportunity

Infometrics forecasts a shortfall of 15,000 accountants within five years, with degree enrolments down 40% since 2018 and a steady brain drain across the Tasman. A NZ$5.1B profession is meeting rising demand with a thinning workforce.

The Thesis

Demand is not the constraint. Capacity is.

The same three forces are reshaping every developed accounting market. Here is how they read in New Zealand.

The Constraint

A pipeline thinning, and talent leaving

  • Infometrics forecasts a shortage of 15,000 accountants within five years1
  • Accounting degree enrolments are down roughly 40% since 20182
  • A sustained brain drain to Australia on a persistent salary gap2
The Demand

A broad base of sole operators

  • 612,420 enterprises, 73% of them non-employing sole operators4
  • A NZ$5.1B accounting services market, growing about 2.2% a year3
  • Demand for advisory and compliance has stayed robust
The Response

Qualified capacity without local poaching

  • Global delivery brings scalable, qualified capacity without competing for scarce locals
  • Judgement, sign-off, and client relationships stay in New Zealand
  • Free senior time for the advisory work clients increasingly want

The Conclusion

Who acts first

The firms that lead the next decade will not necessarily be the firms with the largest offices or the largest local teams. They will be the firms that build scalable operating models, unlock access to talent, and create capacity for growth before their competitors do.

The opportunity already exists. The only question is who acts first.
